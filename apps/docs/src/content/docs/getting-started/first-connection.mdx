This guide walks you through connecting your phone to your Mac for the first time.

**Prerequisites:** Warren installed on your Mac ([Installation](/getting-started/installation/)), phone on the same Wi-Fi.

---

## Step by Step

<Steps>
1. **Start Warren on your Mac.**

   Click the Warren icon in the menu bar. You should see:
   - 🟡 Yellow dot = host mode active, no connections

   If you see ⚫, click the icon and enable **Host Mode**.

2. **Show the QR code.**

   Click the Warren tray icon → **Show QR Code**.

   A window appears with a QR code and a 6-digit numeric PIN (fallback for manual entry).

3. **Open the PWA on your phone.**

   Point your phone's camera at the QR code. It opens `warren.sh` (or your host's local address) in your browser.

   > **No camera?** Tap **Enter PIN manually** and type the 6 digits shown on your Mac.

4. **Approve the pairing on your Mac.**

   A notification appears on your Mac: _"iPhone wants to pair."_ Click **Allow**.

   The tray icon turns 🟢 — a device is now connected.

5. **Open a terminal.**

   In the PWA, your Mac appears in the host list. Tap it.

   A terminal session opens. You're in your Mac's default shell.
</Steps>

---

## What Just Happened

Behind the scenes, Warren:

1. Generated a one-time QR code containing a pairing nonce + your Mac's local IP
2. Your phone sent the nonce back + its X25519 public key (v0.2+)
3. Your Mac verified the nonce, performed ECDH key exchange
4. Both devices derived a shared secret — stored locally, never transmitted
5. Future connections authenticate using this shared secret

In v0.1, the QR code contains a static token (simplified for the proof of concept). Full X25519 pairing arrives in v0.2.

## Troubleshooting

**Phone can't see the QR / can't reach the host**
- Confirm both devices are on the same Wi-Fi network
- Check that Warren's port (default 9470) is not blocked by a firewall
- On the Mac, go to Settings → verify the port matches

**"Connection refused" in the PWA**
- Confirm Warren's host mode is enabled (🟡 or 🟢 tray icon)
- Try entering the host's IP manually: `192.168.x.x:9470`

**Auth failed**
- The token may have expired or been regenerated — show a new QR code and retry

**Terminal appears but input doesn't work**
- The virtual keyboard may be hiding. Tap the terminal to focus it.
- If on iOS, ensure the browser is not in reader mode.
